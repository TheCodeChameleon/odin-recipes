Acarajé

Description

Did you know that the word acarajé originates from the African language Yoruba? In this designation, 'akará' means “ball of fire” and 'jé' means “to eat”.

The dish is produced mostly by women, mothers and daughters of saints. And many times, it is usually offered in candomblé. Despite this, it is not known exactly what the origin of acarajé is, many believe that it has emerged from the Arab part of Africa. What scholars claim is that the dish entered the Brazilian menu after the liberation of slaves.

  • 1 kg black-eyed peas
  • 4 onions
  • salt to taste
  • palm oil to taste
  • 6 chilli pepper
